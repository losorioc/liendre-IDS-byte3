Mobile phone usage during a regular week

The purpose of this exercise is exploring the battery, location, and screen data during an ordinary week of classes and trying to find patterns of usage


I tracked my location, battery charge, and screen from April 2nd to April 12th and tried to find if there was a pattern in the way I charge and use my cellphone during a regular study week.
The three main patterns observed in the data are that a higher battery charge is observed when I'm around Fifth Avenue and Negley or around Forbes Avenue. Also, I found that the distribution in charge levels is similar when I compare class days with weekends. During class days there is a clear seasonality in charge levels, but this pattern is unclear during weekends. Finally, I found that I found that one third of the times that I check my cellphone I do not unlocked it.


As I mentioned before, one third of times I checked my phone I do not unlocked it.


The following map represents every hourly location throughout a blue dot including the label battery charge level. It is possible to filter the data by daytime. It can be morning, evening, afternoon, midnight and midday. Moreover, it is possible to filter by date. For example, if you filter morning, you’ll see that my activity was concentrated around Forbes Avenue, except some dots that correspond to weekends. The level of charge remained higher in locations around the university area (Forbes Avenue). On the contrary, when I filtered the charge levels and location during the evening, the dispersion of the points increases and the charge levels decreases. In part because I don’t have classes after 3 pm.
